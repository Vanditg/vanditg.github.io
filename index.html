About

    My research is in the field of Computer Vision, Deep Learning and Cognitive Vision. Certain questions have led me to this research such as:

    • How human vision works to recognize object/object category with less number of samples?

    • What makes human vision so effective?

    • What can we learn from the human vision?

    Thus intending to find the answers, I do research and develop methods for how to use Cognitive and Computer Vision to benefit each other. I have been also working on understanding vision from person’s perspective, such topics include:

    • Person identification based on the provided description in extremely dense scenes.

    • Human Detection, Pose Estimation and Tracking in surveillance vision.

    • Person Retrieval in Video Surveillance using Semantic Description.

    Therefore in the Past, I have spent time at Ahmedabad University, Meditab Software Inc., Kansas State University, and GTU Robocon Team, where I worked with Hiren Galiyawala, Kenil Shah, Yash Khandhediya, Dwight Day, Kim Fowler and Raj Hakani on several projects.

    For the last two years, I have worked to address the underrepresentation of undergraduates in the field of Computer Vision and have motivated undergraduates in the computing field. To this end, I and my colleague have co-founded Computer Vision Group – LDCE, which focuses on increasing undergraduates knowledge and participation in Computer Vision and Deep Learning Research. Interested to collaborate or join our vision group? Email any of the CVG-LDCE members with the project proposal.

    In my free time, I use Twitter the most and sometimes I also use Instagram to post some of my travel photographs.
